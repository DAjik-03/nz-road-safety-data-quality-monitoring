# Stakeholder Issue Register (Final v1)

## Purpose

This document translates the technical validation and exception review outputs into a concise stakeholder-facing issue register for version 1 of the NZ Road Safety Data Quality & Monitoring Review.

It is intended to sit above the technical validation files and present only the residual issues that are material enough to matter for reporting interpretation, caveat setting, or ongoing monitoring.

---

## Overall Position

The current CAS extract is considered usable for version 1 annual and financial-year monitoring, with targeted caveats rather than broad structural concerns.

The main residual stakeholder-facing caveat is a very small number of records with incomplete geographic reference fields. These records are not expected to materially affect national annual or financial-year summaries, but they do matter for subnational geographic reporting, mapping, and small-area outputs.

A separate isolated historical injury-count exception remains under monitored review only and is not carried forward as a headline stakeholder issue.

## Interpretation Notes

### 1. Why the final register is shorter than the raw exception output

The technical exception review identified 8 low-volume error rows. These are not presented as 8 separate stakeholder issues in the final register.

For stakeholder-facing packaging, they are interpreted into 2 grouped themes:
1. incomplete geographic reference fields
2. a single historical injury-count exception

This keeps the register proportionate to materiality and avoids overstating isolated technical exceptions.

### 2. What should still be considered reliable in v1

The current extract remains suitable for:
- annual monitoring
- financial-year monitoring
- severity and outcome review
- broad reporting-ready quality interpretation

The main caution applies when moving from national or broad aggregate reporting into detailed geography-based outputs.

### 3. What should be caveated

Any reporting that depends directly on:
- TLA-level geography
- area-unit-level geography
- meshblock-level geography
- map-based outputs
- tightly scoped local area interpretation

should include a short note that a very small number of records have incomplete geographic reference fields.
