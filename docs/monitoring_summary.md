# Monitoring Summary (v1)

## Purpose

This document translates the annual and financial-year monitoring summary outputs into short stakeholder-facing monitoring headlines for version 1.

It is intended to show how the quality-monitoring layer can be read in practice, without requiring a stakeholder to inspect the underlying technical summary tables directly.

---

## Overall Monitoring Position

The monitoring outputs support the same overall interpretation reached in the validation and exception-review layers:

**the current extract is usable for version 1 annual and financial-year monitoring, with targeted caveats rather than broad reliability concerns.**

Across both annual and financial-year views, issue-affected records remain low as a share of total records, and error-level records are extremely rare.

Most monitored exceptions sit in the warning layer rather than the error layer, which supports a controlled caveat position rather than a structural failure interpretation.

---

## Annual Monitoring Headlines

### 1. Annual issue rates remain low in percentage terms

Across the annual series, no year exceeds 2.0% of distinct issue-affected records.

The highest annual issue rate is observed in **2019**, with **715 distinct issue records** out of **36,930 total records** (**1.9361%**).

This is the high point in the annual series, but it still remains below 2% of total records.

### 2. Recent complete years are relatively stable

Across the recent complete annual period from **2020 to 2025**, the distinct issue-record rate stays within a fairly narrow range:

- **2020:** 1.1122%
- **2021:** 1.2297%
- **2022:** 1.2544%
- **2023:** 1.2189%
- **2024:** 1.2478%
- **2025:** 1.4041%

This suggests a stable low-volume exception environment rather than a rapidly deteriorating quality position.

### 3. Error-level annual exceptions are rare

Error-level records appear only in a very small number of annual periods:

- **2005:** 1 error record
- **2018:** 1 error record
- **2023:** 3 error records
- **2024:** 1 error record

All other annual rows show **0 error records**.

This reinforces the interpretation that the monitored issue profile is dominated by warnings rather than material structural errors.

### 4. The 2026 annual row should be treated as in-year only

The **2026** annual row contains only **2,940 total records**, which is materially lower than full prior-year volumes.

It should therefore be interpreted as an **incomplete in-year monitoring position**, not as a directly comparable full-year result.

## Financial-Year Monitoring Headlines

### 1. The financial-year view tells the same broad story

No financial year exceeds 2.0% of distinct issue-affected records.

The highest financial-year issue rate is **2018/2019**, with **731 distinct issue records** out of **37,077 total records** (**1.9716%**).

This is the peak financial-year result in the series, but again remains below 2% of total records.

### 2. Recent complete financial years are also stable

Across the recent complete financial-year period from **2019/2020 to 2024/2025**, the distinct issue-record rate remains within a relatively narrow band:

- **2019/2020:** 1.3919%
- **2020/2021:** 1.2135%
- **2021/2022:** 1.2699%
- **2022/2023:** 1.1782%
- **2023/2024:** 1.2255%
- **2024/2025:** 1.3259%

This is consistent with a manageable monitoring profile rather than evidence of broad instability.

### 3. Error-level financial-year exceptions are very rare

Error-level records appear only in a small number of financial years:

- **2004/2005:** 1 error record
- **2018/2019:** 1 error record
- **2022/2023:** 1 error record
- **2023/2024:** 3 error records

All remaining financial-year rows show **0 error records**.

### 4. The 2025/2026 financial year should be treated as in-progress

The **2025/2026** financial-year row contains **17,348 total records**, which is noticeably below the volume seen in completed financial years.

It should therefore be treated as an **in-progress monitoring row**, not as a final full-period comparison point.

---

## Residual Caveat

The monitoring summaries support a usable-with-caveats position, not a clean no-issues position.

The most important remaining caveat is that a very small number of records have incomplete geographic reference fields. These exceptions are unlikely to materially affect national annual or financial-year monitoring, but they matter more for:

- TLA-level reporting
- area-unit reporting
- meshblock-linked interpretation
- map-based outputs
- tightly scoped local-area summaries

---

## Practical Interpretation

For stakeholder-facing reporting, the monitoring outputs support the following practical message:

- the dataset is suitable for annual and financial-year monitoring in version 1
- issue-affected records remain low relative to total record volumes
- error-level exceptions are rare
- the main remaining caution is concentrated in detailed geographic reporting rather than national aggregate reporting

In practical terms, this means the dataset can be presented as:

**fit for version 1 monitoring use, with targeted caveats rather than broad reliability concerns.**
